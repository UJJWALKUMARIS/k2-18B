An Interactive Journey to K2-18b

Exploring a potential 'Hycean' world 120 light-years away.

Discovered in 2015, K2-18b is a super-Earth exoplanet orbiting the red dwarf star K2-18, about 120 light-years away in the constellation Leo. With a mass 8.6 times that of Earth and a radius 2.6 times larger, it falls into the category of "mini-Neptunes." Its orbital period is just 33 Earth days, and it lies in the star’s habitable zone, where liquid water could exist. Observations suggest hydrogen-rich atmospheres, with potential biosignature gases like methane and carbon dioxide detected.

Atmosphere

Studies using the Hubble Space Telescope and JWST suggest K2-18b has a hydrogen-rich atmosphere. Evidence of methane and carbon dioxide has been reported, making it a strong candidate for further exploration.

Habitability

K2-18b orbits in the habitable zone of its star. Its "Hycean" nature (a possible ocean world with hydrogen-rich atmosphere) makes it one of the most intriguing exoplanets in search for alien life.
